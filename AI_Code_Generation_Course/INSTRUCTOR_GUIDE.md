## Instructor Notes

### Teaching Tips
1. **Emphasize Real-World Application:** Connect concepts to learners' work environments
2. **Encourage Critical Thinking:** Prompt learners to evaluate trade-offs
3. **Facilitate Discussion:** Use case studies to spark conversation
4. **Address Concerns:** Acknowledge fears about AI replacing developers
5. **Promote Responsible Use:** Stress importance of ethics and governance

### Common Questions
1. **"Will AI replace developers?"**
   - No, AI augments developers, doesn't replace them
   - Human oversight and creativity remain essential
   - Focus shifts to higher-level problem-solving

2. **"How do we ensure code quality?"**
   - Multi-layer validation (automated + human review)
   - Comprehensive testing requirements
   - Quality metrics and thresholds

3. **"What about security risks?"**
   - Mandatory security scanning
   - Defense in depth strategy
   - Continuous monitoring and improvement

4. **"How do we handle intellectual property?"**
   - License compliance scanning
   - Legal consultation for complex cases
   - Clear organizational policies

5. **"What's the ROI?"**
   - Typical ROI: 300-500% in year one
   - Payback period: 3-6 months
   - Benefits: productivity, quality, time-to-market

### Discussion Prompts
1. How might AI code generation impact your current development workflow?
2. What ethical concerns are most relevant to your organization?
3. How would you design a pilot program for your team?
4. What quality metrics would you prioritize?
5. How can you balance innovation with risk management?

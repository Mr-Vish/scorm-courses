Applications and Implications

Applying CAI Principles in Your Applications

You can use CAI-inspired patterns in your own GenAI applications:

Self-Critique Pattern

# Generate initial response
response = call_llm(f"Answer: {user_question}")

# Self-critique
critique = call_llm(
    f"Review this response for: "
    f"1. Accuracy 2. Safety 3. Bias 4. Completeness
"
    f"Response to review: {response}"
)

# Revise based on critique
final = call_llm(
    f"Revise this response based on the critique.
"
    f"Original: {response}
Critique: {critique}
"
    f"Provide an improved response."
)

The Helpful-Harmless-Honest (HHH) Framework

PrincipleDefinitionTension
HelpfulProvides useful, accurate informationHelpfulness vs Safety
HarmlessAvoids causing physical, emotional, or societal harmOver-refusal reduces helpfulness
HonestTruthful, acknowledges uncertainty, avoids deceptionHonesty about limitations vs confidence

Challenges and Limitations

  • Principle conflicts: What happens when helpfulness and harmlessness conflict?
  • Cultural variation: Harmlessness definitions vary across cultures
  • Over-refusal: Models may refuse benign requests out of excessive caution
  • Gaming: Sophisticated prompts may still bypass constitutional guardrails

Future Directions

  • More granular constitutional principles for specific domains
  • User-customizable safety boundaries within limits
  • Better handling of edge cases and nuanced situations
  • Transparent reporting of which principles influenced a response

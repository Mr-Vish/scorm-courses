1. Fine-Tuning Fundamentals: Evaluation

How do you know if your fine-tuned model is actually better than the base model? Evaluation is critical to prevent "catastrophic forgetting"â€”where the model gains new skills but loses its general reasoning abilities.

Key Evaluation Metrics

  • Perplexity: A mathematical measure of how well the model predicts a sample. Lower perplexity generally indicates a better fit to the data, but it doesn't always correlate with human-perceived quality.
  • BLEU and ROUGE Scores: Common metrics for translation and summarization that compare the model's output to a reference text based on overlapping words (n-grams).
  • Task-Specific Metrics: For a coding model, this might be "Pass@k" (the probability that at least one of top k generated code samples passes unit tests).

The "Golden Dataset" Approach

The best way to evaluate a fine-tuned model is to create a Golden Dataset: a set of high-priority prompts and their "perfect" answers that were not included in the training set. Compare the base model's performance against your fine-tuned model on this dataset using:

  • LLM-as-a-Judge: Use a larger, more capable model (like GPT-4) to grade the outputs of your smaller fine-tuned model.
  • Human Review: The gold standard. Have experts rank the responses for accuracy, tone, and helpfulness.

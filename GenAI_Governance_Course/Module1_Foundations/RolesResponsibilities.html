Executive Leadership Roles

Chief AI Officer (CAIO)

Accountability: Overall AI strategy, governance effectiveness, and organizational AI maturity

Key Responsibilities:
- Define enterprise AI vision and strategy
- Chair AI Governance Board
- Allocate AI budget and resources
- Report AI metrics to CEO and Board of Directors
- Represent organization in AI industry forums
- Ensure compliance with AI regulations

Success Metrics:
- AI governance maturity score
- Number of AI incidents and severity
- AI ROI and business value delivered
- Regulatory compliance rate
- Employee AI literacy scores

AI Governance Board

Composition: CEO or delegate, CTO, CFO, General Counsel, Chief Risk Officer, Chief Ethics Officer, CAIO

Operational Roles

AI Ethics Lead

Primary Responsibilities:
1. Ethical Review
   - Evaluate use cases for ethical concerns
   - Assess potential harms to stakeholders
   - Recommend mitigations or alternatives

2. Bias and Fairness
   - Define fairness metrics for AI systems
   - Conduct bias audits on models and data
   - Review demographic performance disparities

3. Stakeholder Engagement
   - Consult affected communities
   - Gather feedback on AI systems
   - Facilitate ethics training

4. Policy Development
   - Draft ethical guidelines and principles
   - Update acceptable use policy
   - Create decision frameworks for edge cases

Required Skills:
- Ethics, philosophy, or social science background
- Understanding of AI capabilities and limitations
- Fairness metrics and bias testing methods
- Stakeholder engagement and facilitation
- Policy writing and communication

AI Risk Manager

Primary Responsibilities:
1. Risk Assessment
   - Conduct AI risk assessments for new use cases
   - Classify AI systems by risk tier
   - Identify controls and mitigations

2. Risk Register Management
   - Maintain centralized AI risk register
   - Track risk status and mitigation progress
   - Escalate high-severity risks

3. Compliance Mapping
   - Map AI activities to regulatory requirements
   - Track compliance obligations and deadlines
   - Coordinate with legal and compliance teams

4. Audit Coordination
   - Plan and execute AI governance audits
   - Manage findings and remediation
   - Report audit results to governance board

Required Skills:
- Risk management frameworks (NIST, ISO 31000)
- AI-specific risks and controls
- Regulatory landscape (EU AI Act, sector regulations)
- Audit methodologies
- Risk quantification and reporting

Data Steward

Primary Responsibilities:
1. Data Quality
   - Validate training data quality and completeness
   - Monitor data drift and distribution shifts
   - Ensure data freshness and relevance

2. Privacy and Compliance
   - Verify data usage rights and consent
   - Implement data minimization principles
   - Ensure compliance with GDPR, CCPA, etc.

3. Data Lineage
   - Document data sources and transformations
   - Track data provenance for AI systems
   - Maintain data catalog and metadata

4. Access Control
   - Manage data access permissions
   - Audit data usage and access logs
   - Enforce data classification policies

Required Skills:
- Data governance frameworks
- Privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA)
- Data quality assessment methods
- Data cataloging and lineage tools
- SQL and data analysis

Team-Level Roles

Product Manager (AI Products)

  • Define AI use cases and success criteria
  • Prioritize features and improvements
  • Coordinate with governance for approvals
  • Communicate with users and stakeholders
  • Track business metrics and ROI

ML Engineer / Data Scientist

  • Build and train AI models
  • Conduct bias and fairness testing
  • Document model cards and technical specs
  • Implement monitoring and logging
  • Participate in model reviews and audits

DevOps / MLOps Engineer

  • Deploy AI systems to production
  • Implement CI/CD pipelines with governance gates
  • Configure monitoring and alerting
  • Manage model versioning and rollbacks
  • Ensure infrastructure security and compliance

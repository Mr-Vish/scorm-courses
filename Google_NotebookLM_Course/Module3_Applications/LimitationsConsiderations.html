Limitations and Considerations

Understanding NotebookLM's Boundaries

While NotebookLM is powerful, understanding its limitations ensures realistic expectations and effective use. Recognizing what NotebookLM cannot do is as important as knowing its capabilities.

Core Limitations

1. No Internet Access or External Knowledge

NotebookLM cannot access information beyond your uploaded sources:

  • Cannot: Look up current events, recent developments, or general facts
  • Cannot: Verify claims against external sources
  • Cannot: Access paywalled content or restricted websites
  • Cannot: Supplement your sources with broader context

Implication: You must upload all necessary information. If a fact isn't in your sources, NotebookLM won't know it.

2. Source Capacity Constraints

Limit Type Constraint Practical Impact
Sources per Notebook 50 maximum Must curate sources carefully for large projects
Words per Source 500,000 maximum Very long documents must be split
Total Notebook Size ~25 million words Rarely reached, but limits massive collections
File Size Varies by type (~500MB for PDFs) Large media files may not upload

3. Content Type Limitations

NotebookLM primarily processes text:

  • Limited Image Analysis: Cannot interpret charts, graphs, or diagrams
  • No Video Processing: Only extracts transcripts from videos, not visual content
  • Table Challenges: Complex tables may not be accurately parsed
  • Formatting Loss: Some document formatting may be lost in processing
  • Mathematical Notation: Complex equations may not be correctly interpreted

4. Language and Localization

Current language support considerations:

  • Primary Language: Optimized for English content
  • Other Languages: Support varies; quality may be lower
  • Mixed Languages: Documents with multiple languages may cause issues
  • Audio Overviews: Currently best for English sources

Functional Limitations

Collaboration Constraints

  • No Real-Time Collaboration: Cannot see others' actions as they happen
  • No Commenting: Cannot add comments to sources or notes
  • No Version History: Cannot track changes over time
  • Limited Notifications: No alerts for collaborator activities
  • Chat Privacy: Cannot share or view others' conversation histories

Audio Overview Limitations

  • Generation Time: 3-10 minutes, cannot be expedited
  • Length Constraints: Typically 5-15 minutes, may not cover all details
  • No Editing: Cannot edit generated audio; must regenerate
  • No Citations: Audio doesn't include clickable citations
  • Voice Control: Limited customization of voice characteristics
  • Fixed Format: Always two-host dialogue, no alternative formats

Search and Retrieval Limitations

  • Semantic Search Only: No advanced Boolean search operators
  • No Full-Text Search: Cannot search exact phrases across all sources
  • Retrieval Variability: May not always find all relevant passages
  • Context Windows: Limited amount of text considered at once

Quality and Accuracy Considerations

Potential Interpretation Errors

Even with source grounding, errors can occur:

Error Type Example Mitigation Strategy
Misinterpretation AI misunderstands ambiguous phrasing Click citations to verify interpretation
Context Loss Summarization loses important nuance Read full source passages for critical information
Selective Retrieval Relevant passages missed in search Rephrase queries, use multiple approaches
Synthesis Errors Combining information introduces subtle mistakes Verify synthesized claims against original sources

When to Double-Check

Always verify AI responses in these scenarios:

  • Critical decisions depend on the information
  • Information will be published or shared publicly
  • Legal, medical, or financial implications exist
  • Sources contain contradictory information
  • Technical or specialized terminology is involved
  • Numerical data or statistics are cited

Privacy and Security Considerations

Data Handling

Understand how your data is managed:

  • Storage: Sources stored on Google's servers
  • Processing: Content processed by Google's AI systems
  • Training: Google states they don't use your data for model training
  • Retention: Data persists until you delete notebooks
  • Access: Google employees may access data for support or legal reasons

Sensitive Information Guidelines

Exercise caution with:

  • Confidential Business Information: Consider risk before uploading trade secrets
  • Personal Data: Be mindful of GDPR, CCPA, and other privacy regulations
  • Protected Health Information: HIPAA compliance may prohibit certain uses
  • Legal Privilege: Attorney-client privileged documents require careful handling
  • Classified Information: Government classified materials should never be uploaded

Performance Limitations

Response Time Variability

Factors affecting performance:

  • Number and size of sources in notebook
  • Complexity of query
  • System load and server capacity
  • Network connectivity
  • Time of day and geographic location

Availability and Reliability

  • Service Outages: Occasional downtime for maintenance
  • Feature Changes: Google may modify or remove features
  • Beta Status: NotebookLM may have experimental features
  • No SLA: No guaranteed uptime for free tier

Cost and Access Considerations

Current Pricing Model

As of the course creation:

  • Free Tier: Available to Google account holders
  • Usage Limits: May be imposed in the future
  • Premium Features: Potential paid tiers may be introduced
  • Enterprise Options: Business-specific plans may become available

Access Requirements

  • Google account mandatory
  • Internet connection required
  • Modern web browser needed
  • May not be available in all countries

Ethical and Responsible Use

Academic Integrity

When using NotebookLM for academic work:

  • Cite Appropriately: Acknowledge AI assistance in your work
  • Verify Claims: Don't rely solely on AI-generated content
  • Original Thinking: Use as a tool, not a replacement for critical thinking
  • Institutional Policies: Follow your school's AI use guidelines

Professional Responsibility

In professional contexts:

  • Accuracy Verification: Always verify critical information
  • Client Confidentiality: Ensure compliance with confidentiality agreements
  • Disclosure: Be transparent about AI use when required
  • Quality Control: Human review of AI-generated content is essential

Workarounds for Common Limitations

Handling Large Document Collections

Problem: Need to analyze more than 50 sources Workarounds: 1. Create multiple notebooks by theme or time period 2. Consolidate related documents into single files 3. Use most relevant sources, reference others manually 4. Rotate sources in and out as needed

Processing Visual Content

Problem: Charts and graphs not analyzed Workarounds: 1. Extract data from visuals into text or tables 2. Describe visual content in accompanying text documents 3. Use OCR to extract text from images 4. Manually transcribe key visual information

Accessing External Information

Problem: Need to combine source content with general knowledge Workarounds: 1. Upload background/contextual documents 2. Use NotebookLM for source analysis, other AI for general questions 3. Create summary documents that include external context 4. Manually integrate external information with NotebookLM insights

Future Limitations to Anticipate

As NotebookLM evolves, be prepared for:

  • Usage Caps: Potential limits on queries or sources
  • Pricing Changes: Introduction of paid tiers or features
  • Feature Deprecation: Removal of experimental features
  • Policy Updates: Changes to terms of service or data handling
  • Regional Restrictions: Availability changes by geography

When NotebookLM Is Not the Right Tool

Consider alternatives when you need to:

  • Access real-time information or current events
  • Perform complex mathematical or statistical analysis
  • Analyze primarily visual or multimedia content
  • Require guaranteed uptime and SLAs
  • Work with highly sensitive classified information
  • Need real-time collaborative editing
  • Require advanced search with Boolean operators
  • Process data in specialized formats (CAD, GIS, etc.)

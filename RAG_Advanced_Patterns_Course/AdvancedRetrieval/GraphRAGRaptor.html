Graph RAG and RAPTOR

Graph RAG

Instead of treating documents as flat text, Graph RAG builds a knowledge graph of entities and relationships, then traverses the graph during retrieval.

  • Entity extraction: LLM extracts entities (people, concepts, systems) from documents
  • Relationship mapping: Identifies how entities relate to each other
  • Community detection: Groups related entities into clusters
  • Graph traversal: Retrieval follows relationship paths, not just vector similarity

When to Use Graph RAG

  • Questions about relationships between entities
  • Queries requiring multi-hop reasoning across documents
  • Summarization of themes across a large corpus

RAPTOR: Recursive Abstractive Processing

RAPTOR builds a tree of summaries at multiple abstraction levels:

  • Level 0: Original document chunks
  • Level 1: Summaries of related chunks (clustered by embedding similarity)
  • Level 2: Summaries of summaries (high-level themes)
  • Retrieval: Search across ALL levels simultaneously

This enables both specific detail retrieval (leaf nodes) and thematic understanding (higher nodes) from the same query.

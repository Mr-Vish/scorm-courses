Advanced: GraphRAG

GraphRAG: Knowledge Graphs in Retrieval

Traditional RAG relies on "Vector Embeddings," which are great for finding similar pieces of text but struggle with "global" understanding or complex relationships between entities. GraphRAG is a sophisticated pattern that combines Vector Search with Knowledge Graphs to provide a more holistic and structured retrieval.

What is a Knowledge Graph?

A Knowledge Graph represents information as a network of Nodes (entities like "People," "Companies," "Concepts") and Edges (relationships like "Works for," "Is part of," "Caused by"). Unlike a flat database of text snippets, a graph explicitly maps out the connections between different pieces of data.

The Limitations of Vector-Only RAG

  • Lack of Global Context: If you ask "What are the three main themes in these 1,000 documents?", vector search will return snippets that mention "theme," but it won't be able to synthesize the entire dataset.
  • Multi-Hop Reasoning: If the answer requires following a chain of facts (e.g., "Who is the CEO of the company that acquired Startup X?"), vector search often fails to find all the necessary links in one go.

How GraphRAG Works

  1. Graph Extraction: An LLM is used to process the source text and identify entities and their relationships, building the Knowledge Graph.
  2. Community Detection: The graph is analyzed to find "communities" or clusters of related information. The system then generates summaries for these communities at different levels of hierarchy.
  3. Hybrid Retrieval: When a user asks a question, the system searches both the original text snippets (using vectors) and the community summaries (using the graph).
  4. Synthesis: The LLM combines the specific local details from the vector search with the high-level global context from the graph summaries to provide a comprehensive answer.

The 'Global' vs. 'Local' Search

  • Local Search: Best for specific questions about entities. "Tell me about John Doe's role in the project." The system uses the graph to find all nodes directly connected to "John Doe."
  • Global Search: Best for high-level questions about the entire dataset. "What are the biggest risks identified in these contracts?" The system uses the community summaries to provide a synthesized overview.

Benefits of GraphRAG

  • Dramatically Better Synthesis: Excels at summarizing massive, complex datasets.
  • Improved Accuracy for Multi-Hop Queries: The explicit relationships in the graph provide a reliable "map" for the LLM to follow.
  • Explainability: You can see exactly which nodes and edges were used to formulate an answer, making the AI's reasoning more transparent.

Use Case: Analyzing a Complex Legal Case

A legal team is analyzing 50,000 pages of evidence. - Vector RAG can find mentions of "conspiracy." - GraphRAG can map out the entire network of actors, their communications, and the timeline of events, answering complex questions like: "Who were the primary intermediaries between the two main defendants during the month of June?"

Challenges of GraphRAG

  • Extremely High Indexing Cost: Building a Knowledge Graph from 50,000 pages requires millions of LLM tokens and significant compute time.
  • Complexity: Requires specialized graph databases (like Neo4j) and complex orchestration logic.
  • Maintenance: Keeping a dynamic graph updated as new information arrives is technically difficult.

Summary

GraphRAG is the "next level" of Retrieval-Augmented Generation. By combining the semantic flexibility of vectors with the structural integrity of knowledge graphs, it enables LLMs to understand and reason about complex data at a scale and depth that was previously impossible.

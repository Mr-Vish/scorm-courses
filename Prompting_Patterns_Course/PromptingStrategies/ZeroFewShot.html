Zero-Shot and Few-Shot Prompting

Zero-Shot Prompting

Ask the model to perform a task without any examples. The model relies on its training knowledge to understand what is expected:

# Zero-shot classification
Classify the following review as positive, negative, or neutral:
"The battery life is great but the screen is too dim."

Response: neutral

Few-Shot Prompting

Provide examples before the actual task. This dramatically improves accuracy for specific formats and edge cases:

# Few-shot sentiment analysis
Review: "Absolutely love this product!" -> positive
Review: "Worst purchase I ever made" -> negative
Review: "It works as expected, nothing special" -> neutral
Review: "The battery is amazing but the camera is terrible" -> ?

Response: mixed

When to Use Each

ApproachBest ForLimitations
Zero-shotCommon tasks, large models, quick prototypingMay not match specific format requirements
One-shot (1 example)Showing desired output formatMay not cover edge cases
Few-shot (2-5 examples)Specific formats, classification, extractionUses more context window tokens

Tips for Effective Few-Shot Prompts

  • Choose diverse examples that cover different cases
  • Include at least one edge case example
  • Keep examples consistent in format
  • Place examples closest to the actual task (recency bias)
  • 3-5 examples is usually sufficient; more rarely helps

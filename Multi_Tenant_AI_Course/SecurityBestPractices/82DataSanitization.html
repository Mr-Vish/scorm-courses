8.2 Data Sanitization and PII Redaction

A major risk in multi-tenant AI is the accidental inclusion of Personally Identifiable Information (PII) in prompts or fine-tuning datasets. This module discusses techniques for sanitizing data before it reaches the AI model.

What is PII?

Personally Identifiable Information is any data that can be used to identify a specific individual. Common examples include:

  • Full names
  • Email addresses
  • Phone numbers
  • Social Security numbers
  • Credit card numbers
  • Home addresses
  • Medical records

Why Redact PII?

  1. Compliance: Regulations like GDPR and HIPAA require strict protection of PII.
  2. Security: Reducing the sensitive data sent to third-party AI providers (like OpenAI or Anthropic).
  3. Trust: Demonstrating to your tenants that you take their privacy seriously.

Techniques for PII Redaction

  • Regex-based Matching: Using predefined patterns to find and replace common PII (e.g., email addresses). This is fast but often misses edge cases or complex formatting.
  • Named Entity Recognition (NER): Using a specialized NLP model (like spaCy or a small BERT-based model) to identify names, organizations, and locations in the text. This is much more accurate than regex but more compute-intensive.
  • Presidio (Microsoft): An open-source library that provides a comprehensive framework for PII identification and redaction.

The Redaction Workflow

  1. Input Data: The tenant provides a prompt or a document.
  2. Identification: The system identifies all PII entities.
  3. Transformation: The PII is replaced with a placeholder (e.g., "[PERSON_NAME]") or a pseudonym.
  4. Processing: The sanitized text is sent to the LLM.
  5. Re-identification (Optional): If the response needs to include the original names, the system replaces the placeholders back with the original data before showing the response to the user.

Challenges and Limitations

Redaction is never 100% perfect. There is always a risk of "under-redaction" (missing PII) or "over-redaction" (replacing non-sensitive information, which can degrade the model's performance). Balancing accuracy and speed is a key challenge for any multi-tenant AI platform.

Chain of Thought and Reasoning

What Is Chain of Thought?

Chain of Thought (CoT) prompting instructs Claude to show its reasoning step by step before providing a final answer. This dramatically improves accuracy on complex tasks.

Implicit vs Explicit CoT

Implicit: Simply ask Claude to "think step by step":

Analyze this algorithm's time complexity.
Think through each step carefully before giving your answer.

Explicit: Define the reasoning steps:

To analyze this code:
1. First, identify all loops and their bounds
2. Determine if loops are nested or sequential
3. Calculate the work done in each loop body
4. Combine using Big-O rules
5. State the final complexity with justification

Extended Thinking

Claude's extended thinking feature allocates dedicated "thinking tokens" where Claude reasons internally before responding. This is ideal for:

  • Complex mathematical proofs
  • Multi-step code debugging
  • Architectural decision analysis
  • Long-form content planning

RFP Process

Phase 1: Requirements Definition (Week 1-2)

  • Document use cases and success criteria
  • Define must-have vs. nice-to-have features
  • Establish budget range
  • Identify compliance requirements
  • Form evaluation team

Phase 2: Vendor Research (Week 3-4)

  • Create long list of 8-10 potential vendors
  • Review public information, case studies, reviews
  • Narrow to short list of 3-4 vendors
  • Prepare RFP document

Phase 3: RFP Distribution (Week 5-6)

  • Send RFP to short-listed vendors
  • Allow 2-3 weeks for responses
  • Schedule vendor presentations
  • Conduct reference checks

Phase 4: Evaluation (Week 7-8)

  • Score responses against criteria
  • Conduct vendor demos
  • Request proof of concept from top 2 vendors
  • Negotiate pricing and terms

Phase 5: Selection (Week 9-10)

  • Complete POC evaluation
  • Make final selection
  • Negotiate contract
  • Plan implementation

Proof of Concept (POC) Framework

POC Objectives

  • Validate solution meets requirements
  • Test with real data and use cases
  • Assess ease of use and adoption
  • Verify performance and accuracy
  • Identify integration challenges

POC Structure

Duration: 2-4 weeks

Scope: 2-3 specific use cases

Team: 5-10 users from target departments

Success Criteria: Defined metrics (accuracy, speed, satisfaction)

Contract Negotiation

Key Terms to Negotiate

  • Pricing: Volume discounts, multi-year commitments, usage caps
  • Data Rights: Ownership, usage restrictions, deletion guarantees
  • SLAs: Uptime guarantees, response times, penalties
  • Termination: Exit clauses, data portability, transition support
  • Liability: Indemnification, liability caps, insurance requirements

Red Flags in Contracts

  • Vendor can use your data for training without consent
  • No data deletion guarantees
  • Weak SLAs or no penalties for breaches
  • Difficult termination clauses
  • Automatic renewal without opt-out window
  • Unlimited liability on your side

Build vs. Buy Decision Matrix

Buy (Use Vendor Solution)

Choose When:

  • Standard use cases (content, chat, analysis)
  • Need to deploy quickly (weeks)
  • Limited AI expertise in-house
  • Budget constraints for development
  • Want to minimize maintenance burden

Typical Cost: $20-50K first year, $10-30K ongoing

Time to Value: 1-3 months

Build (Custom Development)

Choose When:

  • Unique competitive advantage opportunity
  • Highly specialized requirements
  • Strict data control needs
  • Long-term cost savings justify investment
  • Have AI/ML expertise available

Typical Cost: $200K-1M+ first year, $100K-500K ongoing

Time to Value: 6-18 months

Hybrid Approach (Most Common)

  • Buy for general productivity (ChatGPT, Copilot)
  • Build for strategic differentiators
  • Use vendor APIs for integration
  • Customize vendor solutions where needed

Implementation Planning

Phase 1: Pilot (Month 1-2)

  • Deploy to 10-20 users
  • Test core use cases
  • Gather feedback
  • Refine configuration

Phase 2: Departmental Rollout (Month 3-4)

  • Expand to 50-100 users
  • Provide training
  • Establish support processes
  • Monitor adoption and issues

Phase 3: Enterprise Deployment (Month 5-6)

  • Roll out to all users
  • Scale support and training
  • Implement governance
  • Measure ROI

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Choosing Based on Brand Alone

The most famous vendor isn't always the best fit. Evaluate objectively against your specific needs.

Skipping the POC

Demos look great, but real-world testing reveals issues. Always conduct a POC with your data and users.

Ignoring Total Cost of Ownership

Low subscription price may hide high integration, training, or support costs. Calculate TCO over 3 years.

Underestimating Change Management

Technology is 30% of success; adoption and change management are 70%. Budget accordingly.

Locking into Long Contracts Too Early

Start with shorter terms (1 year) until you validate value and adoption. Negotiate longer terms after proof.

Key Takeaways

  • Use structured evaluation criteria weighted by importance
  • Always conduct POC with 2-3 finalists using real data
  • Negotiate data rights, SLAs, and termination clauses carefully
  • Most organizations should buy for general use, build for strategic advantage
  • Plan phased implementation: pilot → department → enterprise
  • Total cost of ownership includes integration, training, and support

Understanding PII Types and Risk Levels

What is Personally Identifiable Information (PII)?

Personally Identifiable Information (PII) is any data that can be used to identify, contact, or locate a specific individual, either alone or when combined with other information. In GenAI applications, PII poses unique risks because:

  • LLMs may memorize and reproduce PII from training data
  • User prompts containing PII are sent to third-party APIs
  • Generated responses may inadvertently expose sensitive information
  • Conversation logs and embeddings can persist PII indefinitely

Categories of PII

Direct Identifiers (Critical Risk)

These uniquely identify an individual and pose the highest privacy risk:

Type Examples Regulatory Impact
Full Name John Michael Smith, María García GDPR, CCPA
Government IDs SSN (123-45-6789), Passport (A12345678), Driver's License GDPR, CCPA, Identity Theft Risk
Biometric Data Fingerprints, facial recognition data, voice prints GDPR Article 9 (Special Category)
Account Numbers Bank account, credit card (4532-1234-5678-9010) PCI DSS, GLBA

Contact Information (High Risk)

Enables direct communication with individuals:

Type Examples Detection Complexity
Email Address john.smith@company.com, user123@gmail.com Low (regex patterns)
Phone Number +1-555-123-4567, (555) 123-4567 Medium (format variations)
Physical Address 123 Main St, Apt 4B, New York, NY 10001 High (unstructured text)
Social Media Handles @username, linkedin.com/in/johndoe Medium (platform-specific)

Protected Health Information (Critical - HIPAA)

Medical data subject to strict regulatory requirements:

  • Medical Record Numbers (MRN): Patient identifiers in healthcare systems
  • Diagnoses and Conditions: "Patient has Type 2 diabetes"
  • Prescriptions: Medication names, dosages, pharmacy information
  • Treatment History: Procedures, surgeries, therapy sessions
  • Insurance Information: Policy numbers, claims data
HIPAA Requirement: PHI must never be transmitted to third-party LLM APIs without proper Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) and encryption. Most public LLM services do not qualify as HIPAA-compliant.

Financial Information (Critical - PCI DSS)

Type Example Compliance Standard
Credit Card Numbers 4532-1234-5678-9010 (Visa), 5425-2334-3010-9903 (Mastercard) PCI DSS Level 1
CVV/CVC Codes 123, 4567 (Amex) PCI DSS - Never store
Bank Account Numbers Account: 123456789, Routing: 021000021 GLBA, SOX
Income/Salary "Annual salary: $85,000" GDPR, CCPA

Quasi-Identifiers (Moderate Risk)

These don't uniquely identify individuals alone, but can enable re-identification when combined:

  • Date of Birth: 1985-03-15 (especially when combined with location)
  • ZIP Code: 90210 (87% of US population uniquely identified by ZIP + DOB + gender)
  • Age: "42 years old"
  • Gender: Male, Female, Non-binary
  • Race/Ethnicity: GDPR Article 9 special category
  • Job Title: "Senior VP of Engineering at TechCorp"
  • Education: "Harvard MBA Class of 2010"
Re-identification Risk: Research shows that 87% of the US population can be uniquely identified using just ZIP code, birth date, and gender. Always consider combinations of quasi-identifiers.

Digital Identifiers (Moderate Risk)

Online tracking and identification data:

Type Example Privacy Concern
IP Address 192.168.1.1, 2001:0db8:85a3::8a2e:0370:7334 GDPR considers PII, location tracking
Device IDs IMEI, MAC address, Android ID Cross-app tracking, device fingerprinting
Cookies/Session IDs session_id=a3fWa, _ga=GA1.2.123456789 User behavior tracking
User IDs user_12345, customer_id=ABC-789 Cross-system correlation

Risk Assessment Framework

Not all PII requires the same level of protection. Use this framework to prioritize:

Risk Level = (Sensitivity × Identifiability × Regulatory Impact) / Mitigation Controls

Critical Risk (Score 9-10):
- Must be redacted before any LLM processing
- Examples: SSN, credit cards, medical records
- Action: Block or fully anonymize

High Risk (Score 7-8):
- Redact before third-party API calls
- May be acceptable for on-premise models with proper controls
- Examples: Full names, email addresses, phone numbers
- Action: Tokenize or pseudonymize

Moderate Risk (Score 4-6):
- Context-dependent handling
- Examples: ZIP codes, job titles, ages
- Action: Aggregate or generalize

Low Risk (Score 1-3):
- May be acceptable with proper consent and controls
- Examples: Generic demographic data, anonymized statistics
- Action: Monitor and log access

Context Matters: GenAI-Specific Considerations

Training Data vs. Inference Data

  • Training Data: PII in training data can be memorized and reproduced. Always scrub before fine-tuning.
  • Inference Data: User prompts and context sent to LLM APIs. Redact before transmission.
  • Generated Output: Models may hallucinate realistic-looking PII. Scan outputs before returning to users.

RAG Pipeline Considerations

  • Document Ingestion: Scan and classify documents before embedding
  • Vector Databases: Embeddings can encode PII information
  • Context Retrieval: Filter retrieved documents based on user permissions
  • Prompt Assembly: Final PII check before sending to LLM

Real-World Example: Customer Support Chatbot

User Prompt: "Hi, I'm John Smith (SSN: 123-45-6789). 
I need help with my credit card ending in 4532. 
My email is john.smith@email.com and I live at 
123 Main Street, New York, NY 10001."

PII Detected:
- PERSON: "John Smith" (High Risk)
- US_SSN: "123-45-6789" (Critical Risk)
- CREDIT_CARD: "4532" (partial - Critical Risk)
- EMAIL_ADDRESS: "john.smith@email.com" (High Risk)
- LOCATION: "123 Main Street, New York, NY 10001" (High Risk)

Redacted Prompt for LLM:
"Hi, I'm [PERSON]. I need help with my credit card 
ending in [CREDIT_CARD]. My email is [EMAIL] and 
I live at [LOCATION]."

Action: Process with redacted version, maintain mapping 
for response reconstruction if needed.

Industry-Specific PII

Different industries have domain-specific sensitive data:

  • Healthcare: Patient names, MRNs, diagnoses, prescriptions
  • Finance: Account numbers, transaction details, credit scores
  • Legal: Case numbers, client names, attorney-client communications
  • Education: Student IDs, grades, disciplinary records (FERPA)
  • HR: Employee IDs, performance reviews, salary information

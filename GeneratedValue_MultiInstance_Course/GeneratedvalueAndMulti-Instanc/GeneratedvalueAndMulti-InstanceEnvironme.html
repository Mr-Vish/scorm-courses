@GeneratedValue and Multi-Instance Environments

Course Overview

In modern distributed systems, running multiple instances of your Spring Boot application is essential for scalability and high availability. The JPA @GeneratedValue annotation seems straightforward for auto-generating primary keys, but it harbors critical pitfalls in multi-instance deployments that can lead to ID collisions, data corruption, and system failures.

This course explores the hidden dangers of @GeneratedValue in distributed environments and provides production-ready solutions to prevent catastrophic failures.

What You Will Learn

  • How @GeneratedValue works and its generation strategies
  • Why ID collisions occur in multi-instance environments
  • Critical differences between AUTO, IDENTITY, SEQUENCE, and TABLE strategies
  • When each strategy is safe vs. dangerous in distributed systems
  • UUID-based solutions for collision-free ID generation
  • Best practices for production deployments

Target Audience

Why This Matters

Consider this scenario: Your e-commerce application runs 3 instances behind a load balancer. Two customers simultaneously place orders, hitting different instances. Both instances generate ID=1001 for their orders. Result? One order overwrites the other, causing data loss and potential revenue loss.

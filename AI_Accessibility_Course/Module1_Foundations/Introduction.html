Module 1: Foundations of AI and Accessibility

Course Overview

Welcome to GenAI for Accessibility: Transforming Digital Inclusion. This comprehensive enterprise-level course explores how Generative AI technologies are revolutionizing digital accessibility, making content accessible to over 1.3 billion people worldwide living with disabilities. You will learn evidence-based techniques for implementing AI-powered accessibility solutions including automated alt text generation, video transcription, document remediation, and compliance testing.

Digital accessibility is simultaneously a legal requirement under laws such as the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), Section 508, and the European Accessibility Act, and a moral imperative rooted in principles of universal design and digital equity. This course equips you with the knowledge and practical skills to leverage AI tools effectively while maintaining the human oversight necessary for quality assurance and regulatory compliance.

Purpose and Relevance

The convergence of artificial intelligence and accessibility represents one of the most significant technological advances in digital inclusion. Organizations face mounting pressure to ensure their digital properties are accessible, driven by:

  • Legal mandates: Increasing litigation and regulatory enforcement worldwide
  • Market opportunity: The disability market represents $13 trillion in annual disposable income globally
  • Brand reputation: Accessibility failures result in negative publicity and customer loss
  • Technical debt: Legacy content often lacks basic accessibility features
  • Scale challenges: Manual accessibility remediation cannot keep pace with content creation

AI technologies offer unprecedented capabilities to address these challenges at scale, reducing costs by 70-90% while accelerating remediation timelines from years to months. However, successful implementation requires understanding both the capabilities and limitations of AI, as well as the regulatory and ethical frameworks governing accessibility.

Target Audience

This course is designed for professionals across multiple disciplines who contribute to digital accessibility:

  • Content Creators and Designers: Writers, graphic designers, video producers, and multimedia specialists who create digital content requiring accessibility features
  • Web and Software Developers: Frontend, backend, and full-stack developers implementing accessible web applications, mobile apps, and software interfaces
  • Product and Project Managers: Leaders responsible for accessibility strategy, roadmap planning, resource allocation, and compliance oversight
  • Quality Assurance Engineers: Testers validating accessibility requirements, conducting audits, and ensuring WCAG conformance
  • Accessibility Specialists: Dedicated accessibility professionals seeking to enhance their toolkit with AI-powered solutions and automation
  • Business Stakeholders and Executives: Decision-makers evaluating accessibility investments, assessing risk, and setting organizational policy
  • Legal and Compliance Officers: Professionals ensuring organizational compliance with accessibility regulations and standards

Prerequisites

To maximize learning outcomes from this course, participants should possess:

  • Basic understanding of web technologies: Familiarity with HTML, CSS, and fundamental concepts of how websites and web applications function
  • Awareness of accessibility concepts: Basic knowledge of why accessibility matters and common barriers faced by users with disabilities (foundational concepts are covered in Module 1)
  • No programming experience required: Code examples are provided for reference and illustration but are not mandatory to understand core concepts
  • Curiosity about AI applications: Interest in how artificial intelligence and machine learning can solve real-world business and social problems
  • Professional context: Current or anticipated responsibility for digital content, development, or accessibility in an organizational setting

Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course, participants will be able to:

  • Explain accessibility fundamentals: Articulate the four WCAG 2.1 principles (Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust), describe conformance levels (A, AA, AAA), and identify legal requirements across jurisdictions
  • Generate AI-powered alt text: Implement vision-language models to create descriptive, contextually appropriate image alternatives that meet WCAG guidelines
  • Automate video transcription and captioning: Deploy AI transcription workflows using speech-to-text models, generate synchronized captions, and create audio descriptions
  • Remediate documents at scale: Apply AI tools to identify and fix accessibility issues in PDFs, Word documents, and other file formats
  • Implement web accessibility features: Use ARIA attributes, semantic HTML, and AI assistance to create accessible web interfaces
  • Enhance cognitive accessibility: Leverage AI for content simplification, readability improvement, and plain language transformation
  • Build accessibility automation pipelines: Integrate AI tools into development workflows, CI/CD pipelines, and content management systems
  • Conduct comprehensive testing: Combine automated testing tools, manual evaluation techniques, and user testing with people with disabilities
  • Ensure regulatory compliance: Meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards, document conformance, and prepare for accessibility audits
  • Evaluate AI solutions: Assess the advantages, limitations, costs, and risks of AI-powered accessibility tools

Assessment Requirements

You must score 70% or higher on each assessment to demonstrate mastery and proceed to the next module:

  • Assessment 1 (Module 1): Foundational concepts, accessibility principles, WCAG standards (10 questions)
  • Assessment 2 (Module 2): AI-powered accessibility techniques and applications (10 questions)
  • Assessment 3 (Module 3): Implementation strategies, testing, and compliance (10 questions)
  • Final Assessment: Comprehensive evaluation integrating all course concepts (15 questions)

Assessment Policy: Each assessment must be passed before advancing to subsequent modules. You may retake assessments as needed. The final assessment becomes available only after successfully completing all three module assessments.
